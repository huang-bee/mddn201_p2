VIRUSES

A computer virus is designed to spread from host to host and has the ability to replicate itself. 

A virus is a form of malicious code written to alter the way a computer operates and is programmed to spread from one computer to another. A virus operates by inserting or attaching itself to a program or document. It has the potential to cause unexpected damage such as harming the device by corrupting or destroying data.

The Different Types of Viruses

Boot Sector Virus

This type of virus attacks when you start up your computer and spreads through infected USB drives.

Web Scripting Virus

This virus exploists code from web pages and browsers. Accessing these pages can infect your computer.

Direct Action Virus

This type of virus becomes active when you run a file containing a virus, otherwise it remains dormant.

Browser Hijacker

This virus hijacks web browsers to redirect you to dangerous websites.

Macro Virus

This virus spreads when you open an infected document, often through email attatchments.

Resident Virus

This type of virus inserts itself into your computer's memory and can execute at anytime.

What are the Signs of a Virus?

  • Frequent crashes - A virus can inflict damage to your hard drives to make it freeze up and crash.
  • Pop-up Windows - These pop up windows encourage you to visit suspicious websites
  • Unsually slow computer performance - A virus slows down the performance of your computer
  • Unknown programs that start up when you turn on your computer - Viruses install other malicious programs onto your computer
  • Unsual activities like password changes - A virus may be trying to steal your information and lock you out of your computer
  • Changes in your web browser - Strange homepages opening on your browser maygb indicate a virus
  • Mass emails sent form your account - the cyberattacker can take control of your email with a virus

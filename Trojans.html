TROJANS

The name trojan is derived from 'trojan horse'. It is a type of code or software that can gain control of your computer.

Trojans require the user to execute the code by disguising itself as a legitimate software. Once the software is launched and running the malware can disrupt, steal and inflict damage to your system or network.

Trojan Instant Messaging & SMS

This trojan targets instant messaging softwares to steal passwords and login details. If you mobile device is infected, trojan can send and intercept text messages.

Trojan Banker

The aim of this trojan is to steal information from financial accounts. It is designed to steal your online account information which includes banking, credit card and others.

Rootkit Trojan

The rootkit trojan hides or moves files and documents on your computer. The purpose is to extend the time that the trojan program runs on your device.

Fake AV Trojan

This trojan pretends to be an anti-virus software but demands money from you to detect and remove threats. This fake software will most likely not remove any malware on your computer.

Backdoor Trojan

This trojan creates a backdoor to your computer. This allows the cyber attacker to access your computer and control it. Your data is vulnerable and more malware can be uploaded to your device.

Distributed Denial of Service Trojan

Also known as DDosing, this attack takes down networks by flooding it with traffic. The traffic comes from your computer and surrounding devices.

Protect Against Trojans

  • Keep your operating system up to date - cyberattackers tend to exploit security holes in outdated softwares.
  • Be careful with email attachments - if the email seems suspicious it's better to avoid it.
  • Don't visit risky sites - chrome will alert you if the web page you're about to visit is unsafe.
  • Have complex passwords - create a unique password for each account
  • Back up files regularly - If your computer does get infected, you will have a back up.
  • Don't open a link in an email - most trojans attach themselves to these links, only open them if you know exactly what they are
  • Keep personal infromation safe with firewalls - firewalls monitor traffic, blocks trojans and hackers.
